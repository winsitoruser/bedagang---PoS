# Standard Operasional FMCG & Food Retail
## Compliance & Best Practices Guide

## 1. FOOD SAFETY STANDARDS

### A. ISO 22000 - Food Safety Management System
**Requirements:**
- Hazard analysis and risk assessment
- Critical Control Points (CCP) identification
- Monitoring and verification procedures
- Corrective actions and preventive measures
- Traceability system implementation
- Documentation and record keeping

**Implementation in System:**
- ✅ Batch/lot tracking for full traceability
- ✅ Quality control checkpoints
- ✅ Waste categorization by risk level
- ✅ Complete audit trail
- ✅ Corrective action recording

### B. HACCP (Hazard Analysis Critical Control Points)
**7 Principles:**
1. Conduct hazard analysis
2. Determine Critical Control Points (CCPs)
3. Establish critical limits
4. Establish monitoring procedures
5. Establish corrective actions
6. Establish verification procedures
7. Establish record-keeping procedures

**Critical Control Points in Production:**
- Raw material receiving (temperature, quality)
- Cooking/processing (time, temperature)
- Cooling (temperature, time)
- Packaging (seal integrity, contamination)
- Storage (temperature, humidity)

### C. GMP (Good Manufacturing Practices)
**Key Areas:**
- Personnel hygiene and training
- Facility design and maintenance
- Equipment sanitation
- Process controls
- Quality assurance
- Waste management

## 2. INVENTORY MANAGEMENT STANDARDS

### A. FIFO (First In, First Out)
**For Non-Perishables:**
- Oldest stock used first
- Prevents obsolescence
- Reduces waste from expiry
- Standard for dry goods

**Implementation:**
- Date-based stock rotation
- Automated FIFO alerts
- Warehouse layout optimization
- Regular stock audits

### B. FEFO (First Expired, First Out)
**For Perishables:**
- Shortest shelf life used first
- Critical for food safety
- Mandatory for refrigerated/frozen items
- Prevents expired product sales

**Implementation:**
- Expiry date tracking
- Automatic prioritization
- Near-expiry alerts (30/15/7 days)
- Batch segregation

### C. Batch/Lot Management
**Requirements:**
- Unique batch identifier
- Production date and time
- Expiry date calculation
- Raw material batch linkage
- Complete traceability

**Benefits:**
- Rapid recall capability
- Quality issue isolation
- Supplier accountability
- Regulatory compliance

## 3. QUALITY CONTROL STANDARDS

### A. Incoming Quality Control (IQC)
**Raw Materials:**
- Visual inspection
- Temperature check (cold chain)
- Expiry date verification
- Supplier certificate review
- Sample testing (if required)
- Accept/reject decision

### B. In-Process Quality Control (IPQC)
**During Production:**
- Process parameter monitoring
- Visual inspection at each stage
- Weight/volume verification
- Temperature control
- Contamination prevention
- Hygiene compliance

### C. Final Quality Control (FQC)
**Finished Products:**
- Appearance (color, shape, size)
- Taste/flavor profile
- Texture/consistency
- Packaging integrity
- Label accuracy
- Metal detection (if applicable)
- Microbiological testing (sampling)

### D. Quality Grading System
**Grade A (Premium):**
- Perfect quality
- Full price
- Normal distribution

**Grade B (Second):**
- Minor defects
- Cosmetic issues only
- Safe for consumption
- Clearance sale (50-70% price)

**Grade C (Reject):**
- Major defects
- Not safe or not acceptable
- Disposal required
- No sale allowed

## 4. WASTE MANAGEMENT PROTOCOLS

### A. Waste Segregation
**Categories:**
1. **Food Waste (Organic)**
   - Expired products
   - Production waste
   - Spoiled items
   - Composting potential

2. **Packaging Waste**
   - Cardboard/paper
   - Plastic wrapping
   - Glass/metal containers
   - Recycling program

3. **Hazardous Waste**
   - Cleaning chemicals
   - Contaminated materials
   - Special disposal required

### B. Disposal Methods
**1. Landfill Disposal:**
- For non-recyclable waste
- Documented weight/volume
- Licensed disposal contractor
- Certificate of disposal

**2. Composting:**
- Organic food waste
- Environmental benefit
- Cost reduction
- Sustainability report

**3. Animal Feed:**
- Safe food waste
- Quality verification
- Licensed recipient
- Documentation required

**4. Incineration:**
- Contaminated products
- Recall items
- High-risk waste
- Witnessed destruction

**5. Donation:**
- Near-expiry but safe
- Food bank partnership
- Tax benefit
- Documentation for CSR

### C. Documentation Requirements
**For Each Waste Event:**
- Waste number/ID
- Date and time
- Product details (name, batch, expiry)
- Quantity and weight
- Reason for disposal
- Disposal method
- Witness signature
- Photo evidence
- Certificate of destruction (if applicable)

## 5. FINANCIAL ACCOUNTING STANDARDS

### A. Cost Accounting
**Production Costing:**
```
Total Production Cost = Direct Materials + Direct Labor + Manufacturing Overhead
Cost per Unit = Total Production Cost / Good Units Produced
Waste Cost = Defective Units × Cost per Unit
```

**Variance Analysis:**
- Material usage variance
- Yield variance
- Scrap variance
- Efficiency variance

### B. Inventory Valuation
**Methods:**
- FIFO (First In, First Out)
- Weighted Average Cost
- Standard Costing

**Adjustments:**
- Obsolescence provision
- Slow-moving provision
- Damage provision
- Expiry provision

### C. Journal Entries

**1. Normal Waste (Within Tolerance):**
```
Dr. Cost of Goods Sold           Rp XXX
   Cr. Inventory - Raw Materials      Rp XXX
```

**2. Abnormal Waste (Above Tolerance):**
```
Dr. Abnormal Loss Expense        Rp XXX
   Cr. Inventory - Raw Materials      Rp XXX
```

**3. Defective Products (Rework):**
```
Dr. Work in Process - Rework     Rp XXX
   Cr. Inventory - Finished Goods     Rp XXX
```

**4. Defective Products (Scrap Sale):**
```
Dr. Cash/Accounts Receivable     Rp XXX
Dr. Loss on Scrap Sale           Rp XXX
   Cr. Inventory - Finished Goods     Rp XXX
```

**5. Expired Products (Write-off):**
```
Dr. Inventory Loss - Expired     Rp XXX
   Cr. Inventory - Finished Goods     Rp XXX
```

**6. Product Recall:**
```
Dr. Product Recall Expense       Rp XXX
```

## 12. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

### A. Product Recall
**Immediate Actions:**
1. Stop distribution immediately
2. Identify affected batches
3. Notify customers/retailers
4. Retrieve products from market
5. Segregate and quarantine
6. Investigate root cause
7. Implement corrective actions
8. Document entire process
9. Report to authorities
10. Review and improve system

### B. Contamination Event
**Response Protocol:**
1. Isolate affected area
2. Stop production
3. Secure evidence
4. Notify management
5. Assess extent of contamination
6. Determine affected products
7. Initiate recall if needed
8. Clean and sanitize
9. Verify before restart
10. Document and report

### C. Equipment Failure
**Contingency Plan:**
1. Switch to backup equipment
2. Assess product safety
3. Quarantine questionable products
4. Repair or replace equipment
5. Verify calibration
6. Resume production
7. Document incident
8. Review maintenance schedule
